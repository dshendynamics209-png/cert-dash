ISACA mindset
Re-read before every practice Q session and mock exam
You are the 2nd line of defence
You evaluate, guide, and oversee risk decisions — you do not directly fix systems.
Answer at the enterprise level, not as a StatCan SA&A analyst.
The correct answer manages risk long-term, not the fastest one operationally.
Governance and risk ownership always come before controls and technical solutions.
Readiness gate
✦ Explain WHY the correct answer is correct for 80%+ of questions, not just select it
✦ Questions you guess correctly are treated as wrong — articulate the rationale
✦ Eliminate 2 wrong answers confidently within 30 seconds on most questions
Wrong-answer trap taxonomy — label each incorrect answer T1–T6
T1 — Chose a technical fix instead of a governance action
T2 — Answered for my job (GC/StatCan) instead of "ideal enterprise"
T3 — Picked fastest/cheapest instead of most risk-appropriate
T4 — Ignored risk ownership — who approves, who is accountable
T5 — Focused on controls before identifying and scoping the risk
T6 — Skipped escalation or communication — acted unilaterally
Answer-elimination heuristic
Eliminate any option that: has no clear risk owner · is a quick technical fix (patch/block/disable) · ignores governance · solves the symptom not the root risk · skips communication or escalation · uses line-1 language ("IT will fix it") instead of line-2 ("risk owner will assess it")
Key vocabulary — concepts before frameworks
Inherent risk — risk before controls
Residual risk — risk after controls
Risk appetite — strategic, board-level
Risk tolerance — operational threshold
Risk capacity — max absorb before mission failure
Risk owner — approves residual risk (not IT)
KRI — forward-looking risk signal
KCI — control health · KPI — business performance
Lines of Defence (2025 term) — CRISC practitioner sits in line 2
